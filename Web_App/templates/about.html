FALCON Chatbot
The FALCON Team

We are a team of four high schoolers (Zenchang, Kenneth, Justin, and Niko) in the Applied Physics Laboratory's ASPIRE program. Over the course of the entire summer (2023), we created the FALCON chatbot, short for Fine-tuning Advanced Language models for Cross-domain OptimizatioN.

The Origins of FALCON

There are a number of issues with current, publicly available chatbots—some examples being OpenAI's ChatGPT and Microsoft's Bing Chat—which prevent realistic deployment into secure engineering environments. For example, these models have no knowledge of events beyond the time from which they were trained, meaning that developers won't be able to use these resources for information on new packages and technologies. Additionally, these models, while capable of producing general information on a wide range of topics, struggle to answer specific questions on more specialized domains. To combat these issues, we wanted to implement our very own chatbot which addresses the issues and which is shipped with built-in guardrails and self-explanation tools to enhance robustness and trust.

How it was Built
  • Original model: GPT-J (6 billion parameters)
  • Fine-tuned on: radar textbooks and journals, briefs
  • Protected with: Nemo Gaurdrails (NSFW, jailbreaking)
  • Built with: LangChain Human Machine Teaming capabilities
  • Constructed with: Flask (Python web framework)
